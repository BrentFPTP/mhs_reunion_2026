Good to know

Fully casual — no dress code. Stays are all-inclusive: nightly chef's dinner & breakfast, and it's non-tipping. Cabins are intentionally screen-free (no Wi-Fi, TV, or phones) — Wi-Fi is in the Lodge if you need it.

Costs & Payment

How the money works

This is a no-host event. You book and pay the lodge directly for your own cabin — the reunion team isn't collecting or holding anyone's money.

  • Your cabin stay (paid to the lodge) includes nightly chef's dinner & breakfast, plus paired wine & craft beverages.
  • Orchard Canyon is a non-tipping resort — no gratuity expected.
  • A deposit equal to one night's stay reserves your cabin; the lodge arranges that with you directly.
  • Possible add-on: we may organize one catered group lunch — [optional, extra cost TBD].
  • Everything else (travel, meals out, activities, shopping) is each guest's own.

Pay the venue directly

Everyone's Invited

Staying elsewhere? You're still in.

With only 17 cabins on the property, there's a real chance we'll have more classmates than beds — and that is completely okay.

No one gets left out. 💚💛

If you end up not staying at the lodge — whether the cabins fill up, you found a better deal, or you just prefer your own spot — you are 100% welcome to spend the days with us at Orchard Canyon.

Come hike the red rocks, cool off in the creek, play lawn games, gather by the firepit, and join the get-togethers. Being a "day guest" doesn't mean missing out on the reunion.

One detail we're sorting out: the lodge's dinners & breakfasts are tied to cabin stays, so [we'll confirm day-guest meal options & any charge with Orchard Canyon]. Either way, the daytime fun is open to all.

Local Guide

Sedona & Oak Creek Canyon

Your meals are covered at the lodge, so these are picks for lunches and exploring — plus the iconic red-rock sights and shopping.

The Hudson

★ 4.6
Elevated casual American · views

A Sedona favorite with red-rock views and a lively patio. Reservations recommended. Great for a meal out.

Wildflower

★ 4.5
Cafe · breakfast & lunch

Order-at-the-counter spot with big views, fresh bowls, sandwiches, and great coffee — easy lunch before a hike.

Hideaway House

★ 4.5
Italian & pizza · creekside patio

Rustic Italian on the hillside with a cozy patio over Oak Creek. Try the brick chicken or a peach pizza.

The Vault Uptown

★ 4.5
Bar & grill · uptown

Huge portions, steaks and smash burgers, and a great uptown patio view. Good for a casual crowd.

Slide Rock State Park

★ 4.7
Natural water slide · minutes away

A natural sandstone water chute in Oak Creek, just up 89A from the lodge. Bring water shoes — rocks are slick, water's brisk. Perfect for June.

Devil's Bridge

★ 4.8
Iconic hike · moderate

Sedona's most photographed sandstone arch. Go early to beat crowds and heat. ~4 mi round trip from Mescal trailhead.

Oak Creek Vista

★ 4.8
Scenic overlook · top of the canyon

Sweeping canyon views at the top of the 89A switchbacks, plus Native American artisan vendors. An easy, dramatic stop.

Crescent Moon / Red Rock Crossing

★ 4.7
Cathedral Rock · creek swimming

The classic Cathedral Rock reflection view with shady creekside spots to wade and picnic. ~$15/vehicle.

Tlaquepaque Arts & Shopping Village

★ 4.6
Galleries & boutiques

A gorgeous Spanish-style village of courtyards, fountains, galleries, and shops. Worth a stroll even if you don't buy a thing.

Uptown Sedona

★ Shops & gifts
Walkable district

Souvenirs, Southwestern art, jewelry, and ice cream along 89A, with red-rock views at every turn.

Oak Creek Vista vendors

★ Local crafts
Native American artisans

Handmade jewelry and pottery at fair prices, right at the canyon overlook — an easy stop coming or going.
